
The HL7eu and IHEeu imaging report group has finished processing the ballot comments of the [0.1.0 ballot](https://hl7.eu/fhir/imaging-r5/0.1.0-ballot/). Besides addressing these ballot comments we also added and R4 version of the specification. 

Both specifications are generated from a common source base and reflect the same content. The ballot candidate can be found [HL7 Europe Imaging Report R4 v1.0.0-ballot](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/imaging-r4/branches/ballot-0.2.0/en/).

### Ballot instructions

This specification currently under ballot:
* Ballot period: March 23rd – April 30th, 2026
* Implementation Guide: https://hl7.eu/fhir/imaging/1.0.0-ballot/
* Ballot type: STU1 Ballot (1.0.0-ballot)
* Submit comments (HL7 Jira): https://jira.hl7.org/
* Commenting instructions: https://confluence.hl7.org/x/4AAkAQ

Participation:
* Who can comment?
  * Anyone may submit comments via HL7 Jira. Community feedback is strongly encouraged.
  * Comments on the EU Imaging Study Report FHIR Implementation Guide (R5) (https://hl7.eu/fhir/imaging-r5/1.0.0-ballot/) are also encouraged
* Who can vote?
  * Only designated voters from HL7 Europe affiliates may cast official ballot votes. 
  * Designated voting members are the HL7 Europe Technical Network Team (TNT) members, or the national affiliate chair (or their deputy) if a country is not represented in the TNT.

About this Implementation Guide:
* The EU Imaging Study Report FHIR Implementation Guide specifies the representation of Imaging Study Reports in the European context as a FHIR-based model.

It defines:
* The structure and content of FHIR Imaging Study Reports
* Mappings to the Xt-EHR logical models for imaging reports
* Example implementations of the defined models
* The relationship with existing interoperability frameworks, including IHE MADO, IHE RAD, and EHDS API specifications

We kindly invite you to:
* Review the Implementation Guide
* Submit comments via HL7 Jira
* Cast your ballot if you are a designated voter
* Share this announcement within your national affiliate and relevant communities

Your participation is essential to ensure high-quality, interoperable FHIR specifications for Europe.
Thank you very much for your support and contributions.
